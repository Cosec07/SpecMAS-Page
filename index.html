SpecMAS turns temporal-logic specifications into trustworthy, adaptive coordination policies for teams of autonomous agents. The framework couples verifiable controllers with data-driven skill refinement to stay reliable in dynamic environments.

Overview

SpecMAS unifies formal synthesis with lightweight learning updates so multi-agent teams can plan, execute, and recover from disruptions while satisfying mission requirements.

The system begins with probabilistic signal temporal logic that captures high-level safety and collaboration rules. These specifications are compiled into policies that remain verifiable even as the environment changes. Runtime monitors supervise execution and trigger replanning or recovery strategies when agents drift from the mission envelope.

  • Specification-guided planning. Mission objectives become temporal-logic controllers that encode fairness, safety, and communication guarantees.
  • Adaptive skill library. Agents refine reusable skills with contrastive imitation learning for quick transfer between simulators and real robots.
  • Verified coordination. Runtime monitors enforce guarantees and initiate recovery behaviors during unexpected events.
